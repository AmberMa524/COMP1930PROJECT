Peter Bond
I've been using this for about a week now and so far it's great. While it's technically not a gaming laptop it will handle most newer games at medium to high settings with decent frame rates. The screen has great color and brightness, sound despite bottom facing speakers was louder than expected. The track-pad is responsive and snappy (no physical buttons) >which might be a con to some. Battery life while watching netflix and surfing the web has been anywhere from 4 1/2 to 5 1/2 hours for me. I'm sure I could get more with some tweaking.
I did have to order a mounting bracket and cable from Acer to attach my hdd (part # 42.GP4N2.SV1) that you can order from the Acer website for $8 usd. Other reviews have mentioned you can call them and see if they'll send you one for free. I opted to forgo the hassle and pay the 8 bucks.
Pros: A lot of power packed into a $600 laptop. If you're not a hardcore gamer but still like to play games this might work for you. It exceeded my expectations.
Cons: (These are nit picky and subjective)
1. No cd drive but who uses those anymore anyway. If you do need one you can always plug a cheap usb one into it.
2. If you want to add a 2.5 hdd for storage you need to order the cable and bracket along with being comfortable
opening up a brand new laptop. The Acer website has a guide with pictures on how to do it, though.
3. Plastic lid and aluminum palm rest show prints fairly easily. If you have ocd like me that might bother you but pretty minor.
Over all very pleased with this purchase. You'd be hard pressed to find a laptop with 256g ssd, discrete graphics, i5 8250u
processor and ddr4 memory for the money. (At the time of this review) Will update this in the future should my opinion change.
